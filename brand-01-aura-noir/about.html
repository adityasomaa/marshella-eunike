The Beginning

A grandmother's recipe, re-translated.

AURA NOIR was founded in 2014 by Ines Wijaya, a former art-direction consultant who returned to Jakarta after a decade abroad and found herself, of all places, in her late grandmother's pantry. Among the rice tins and dried bunga melati, she found a notebook — a soft, water-stained ledger of skin recipes scribbled in three languages.

One formula was titled, in Javanese script, "For the face, after midnight." It contained tamanu, kemiri, sea salt, and a single drop of agarwood. She made the first bottle that same week. It is what we now call the Noir Élixir.

A decade later, the maison has grown into three small ateliers — Jakarta, Bali, and a partnership distillery in Marrakech — but every formula still begins on that same notebook, on a table, by hand. Slowly.

Filosofi Maison

The four slow rules.

There are only four. They were written on the back of an envelope, the night the maison was named. They have not changed since.

01

Time is the most expensive ingredient.

Every formula at AURA NOIR rests for at least forty-two days. Some, for ninety. We do not ship until the bottle is ready to be opened. There is no calendar in the laboratory; there is only the smell of a batch becoming itself.

02

Hands, never machines, finish a bottle.

Filling, capping, sealing, signing — every final gesture belongs to a person. There are nine of us in the Jakarta atelier. Each of our names appears, in golden ink, on the bottles we have personally closed.

03

Make less than is asked for.

Editions are intentionally small. When an edition sells out, it does not return; the next one is composed slightly differently, in honor of the season it was made in. Beauty, like a vintage, should remember its year.

04

Leave a smaller bottle than the one you took.

Every formula is refillable. Glass is mouth-blown in Yogyakarta, never single-use. Cartons are 100% recycled paper, printed with soy-based ink. The whole atelier is solar-warmed during daylight hours.
